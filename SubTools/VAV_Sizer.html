How to Select — 2,000 FPM vs 80% of Max

Two methods set how much air you push through a given inlet. Remedy recommends the 2,000 FPM selection over sizing to 80% of the box maximum.

Preferred · 2,000 FPM
Sizes the box to a constant 2,000 FPM inlet velocity. Lower velocity means lower pressure drop, less noise, and steadier control — so the box is much easier to balance. Use this by default.
80% of 1.5″ Max
Pushes air to 80% of the box's full-open (1.5″ w.g.) maximum. It fits a smaller, cheaper box, but runs at higher velocity — more noise, more pressure drop, and harder to balance.
Don't spec below Size 6. Sizes 4, 5 and 6 are the same physical box — the only difference is that the 4″ and 5″ inlets ship with a duct reducer, which just hurts us when the system is being balanced. When the math lands on a 4 or 5, use Size 6 instead. A smaller size can work if you genuinely need its lower minimum, but Size 6 is preferred.
